# Learning / Human Performance Trajectory Lane

Date created: 2026-06-27
Status: Draft v0.1
Audience: Internal / education-safe / human-performance-safe
Scope: Observable-only learning and performance trajectory review with privacy, consent, and non-diagnostic boundaries.

## One-Sentence Summary

This lane explores whether observable learning or human-performance trajectories can support candidate-discrepancy memos for human educators, coaches, or reviewers without making diagnostic, disciplinary, or automated placement decisions.

## Purpose

The learning and human performance lane adapts SpecShift-style trajectory review to education, training, and performance-support settings.

The goal is to help human reviewers inspect visible learning or performance pathways.

The goal is not to diagnose students, replace educators, assign disability labels, automate discipline, or make high-stakes placement decisions.

## Corrected Status

This lane is exploratory.

It has not been validated as an educational intervention, diagnostic instrument, disability screener, clinical tool, or human-performance optimizer.

Any future pilot must be privacy-first, consent-aware, human-controlled, and institutionally governed.

## Core Framing

Learning and performance are trajectories.

A final score, completion marker, or performance output may hide important process information.

A learner may reach an answer through unstable, unsupported, copied, rushed, avoidant, or inconsistent steps.

A trainee may complete a task while visible workflow data suggests unresolved strain or skipped checkpoints.

The review question is:

> Given observable learning or performance traces, does the visible trajectory support the claimed mastery, completion, readiness, or performance state?

## Candidate Use Cases

Possible bounded use cases:

- Assignment completion trajectory review.
- Skill acquisition pathway review.
- Training module completion review.
- Practice-to-performance transition review.
- Tutoring session workflow review.
- Adaptive learning sequence review.
- Coaching feedback loop review.
- Human-in-the-loop AI tutoring review.
- Professional training simulation review.
- Certification-prep workflow review.

Each pilot should be scoped to one bounded learning or performance workflow.

## Observable Trace Inputs

Possible inputs include:

- Timestamped learning events.
- Task attempts.
- Revision history.
- Hints requested.
- Checkpoint completion.
- Practice sequence.
- Assessment item sequence.
- Tutor or coach feedback events.
- Self-reflection entries where voluntarily included.
- Training simulation steps.
- Completion markers.
- Error/retry patterns.
- Observable support-seeking behavior.

Do not require private thoughts, hidden cognition, medical data, disability records, or protected student information beyond the governed pilot scope.

## What This Lane Could Return

A future review output may identify candidate discrepancies such as:

- Claimed mastery unsupported by visible practice trajectory.
- Completion marker inconsistent with skipped checkpoints.
- Sudden performance jump needing human review.
- Repeated error pattern masked by final completion.
- Training readiness claim unsupported by simulation path.
- Support need visible in trajectory but absent from final report.
- Ambiguous case requiring educator, coach, or reviewer adjudication.

The output is a candidate memo, not a final judgment.

## Privacy and Consent Boundary

Learning and performance data can be sensitive.

Any pilot must address:

- Consent where required or appropriate.
- Data minimization.
- Student or participant privacy.
- De-identification where possible.
- Clear purpose limitation.
- Retention limits.
- Access control.
- Human oversight.
- Institutional approval.
- Applicable education, privacy, labor, and civil rights requirements.

Do not casually move identifiable student or performance data into external systems.

## Education-Specific Boundary

For school contexts, this lane must preserve human educator control.

Do not use it for:

- Automated special education eligibility.
- Automated IEP decisions.
- Automated disciplinary referrals.
- Automated grading without teacher review.
- Disability inference.
- Mental health screening.
- Teacher evaluation without governance.
- Student surveillance.

## Human Performance Boundary

For workplace, training, or coaching contexts, this lane must avoid becoming covert surveillance.

Do not use it for:

- Secret worker monitoring.
- Automated firing or discipline.
- Medical inference.
- Psychological profiling.
- Productivity punishment.
- Rights-violating surveillance.
- Unconsented biometric or behavioral tracking.

## Pilot Design

A safe pilot should include:

1. One bounded learning or performance workflow.
2. Approved observable traces.
3. Clear privacy and consent boundaries.
4. Human-retained labels.
5. Human-controlled ground truth or adjudication.
6. Pre-registered success criteria.
7. False-positive accounting.
8. False-negative accounting.
9. Ambiguous-case handling.
10. Baseline comparison where available.
11. Educator, coach, or reviewer control.
12. No diagnostic or disciplinary authority.
13. No protected method disclosure.
14. No high-stakes automated decision-making.

## Label Categories

Preferred label categories:

- Confirmed trajectory discrepancy.

- Out of scope.
- Excluded from scoring.

Optional sublabels:

- Practice-performance mismatch.
- Support need indicator.

- Ambiguous learning path.
- Human review required.

## Pre-Registered Success Criteria

A pilot may evaluate:

- Precision of candidate discrepancy flags.
- Recall against human-labeled discrepancies.
- False-positive rate.
- False-negative rate.
- Educator or reviewer usefulness.
- Ability to surface cases needing support.
- Time saved in review.
- Quality of trajectory-grounded explanation.
- Comparison against existing review or support process.

Do not define success after seeing outputs.

## Minimum Acceptance Template

> The pilot is successful if the review process produces candidate trajectory-discrepancy memos that meet pre-registered usefulness and error-accounting thresholds on human-retained labels, without making diagnostic, disciplinary, placement, or high-stakes automated decisions.

## Safe ΔΔF Relationship

ΔΔF may be used as a public intuition for second-order change in learning or performance trajectories.

Safe version:

> F(t) shows visible learning or performance behavior. ΔF shows how that behavior changes. ΔΔF may highlight stress, instability, or transition pressure in the change pattern.

Do not claim that ΔΔF reveals hidden cognition.

Do not claim that ΔΔF diagnoses learning disability, mental health, motivation, or ability.

## Baseline Comparison

Where available, compare against:

- Existing educator review.
- Current coaching review process.
- Human tutor review.
- Existing learning analytics dashboard.
- Current training assessment workflow.
- Manual review without SpecShift memo.
- Null baseline.

Use the same governed dataset and human-retained labels where possible.
