## Origin Frame

The ∆∆F corpus developed a recurring insight:

> First-order outcomes can look acceptable while second-order trajectory structure reveals stress, drift, replay, forcing, or loss of organized correction.

SpecShift translates that insight into a practical review posture:

> Review the observable path, not only the final answer.

## What Transfers from ∆∆F to SpecShift

Transferable:

- first-order success is insufficient
- trajectories matter
- perturbation and null thinking matter
- final-state claims should be checked against process evidence
- reviewer-controlled adjudication matters
- non-claims and falsifiers keep the system honest

Not transferable as public/commercial proof:

- physics-adjacent claims
- consciousness claims
- ownership metaphysics
- warp / vacuum / exotic-energy material
- unvalidated production predictions
- claims of hidden mechanism access

## Safe SpecShift Sentence

> SpecShift applies the ∆∆F design instinct in a buyer-safe form: it examines observable workflow trajectories for candidate discrepancies between the path taken and the final state claimed.

## Shorter Version

> ∆∆F is the provenance. SpecShift is the review product.

## Boundary

SpecShift should stand on:

- buyer pain
- observable traces
- blind audit design
- candidate-discrepancy memos
- human adjudication
- external validation

Not on:

- speculative physics
- metaphysical agency
- untested production claims
- internal mechanism inference
