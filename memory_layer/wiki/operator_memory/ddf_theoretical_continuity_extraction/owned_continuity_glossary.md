# Owned Continuity Glossary

Date: 2026-06-27  
Status: internal extraction draft  
Source group: continuity / ownership / separability documents from the 18-document ∆∆F corpus

## Purpose

This glossary keeps continuity language usable without overclaiming.

## Terms

### ∆∆F / κ

Observable second-order trajectory structure.

Safe use:

> A curvature-like statistic over a time-indexed signal.

Avoid:

> A direct measure of consciousness or intent.

### Performance

Whether a system succeeds at a task or preserves a desirable output.

Examples:

- reward
- accuracy
- final answer
- ledger state
- task completion

Risk:

Performance can persist under replay, forcing, or environmental simplicity.

### Stability

Whether a system’s visible signal remains smooth, bounded, or low-error.

Risk:

Stability can be externally imposed or passively maintained.

### Continuity

Persistence of structured correction across time and perturbation.

Safe use:

> A diagnostic construct for whether correction structure remains organized under stress.

Avoid:

> Proof of identity or selfhood.

### Owned continuity

Persistence of stable, endogenous, recurrent, state-contingent correction structure under perturbation.

Safe use:

> A bounded theoretical construct.

Avoid:

> Consciousness, personhood, or moral-status claim.

### Endogenous organization

A correction pattern that depends on the system’s own state, feedback, or update coupling rather than being imposed from outside.

### State-contingent correction

Adjustment that changes depending on the system’s current state or context.

Failure contrast:

- state-independent forcing
- replay
- frozen policy
- scripted sequence

### Recurrence

Persistence or repeated structure in the curvature signal across time.

Safe use:

> A time-series property that may support a continuity interpretation when paired with null testing.

### Scrambled-control null

A surrogate test that preserves broad marginal statistics while disrupting ordering or state-action coupling.

### Replayed control

A control sequence that reproduces behavior without live adaptation.

### Passive stability

Smooth or bounded behavior that does not require persistent correction.

### Active stability

Stability maintained through ongoing correction.

### Transition regime

A regime where first-order stability or second-order regularity breaks down, suggesting reconfiguration or loss of prior organization.

## Master Caution

Continuity language is powerful and risky.

Use it as a technical diagnostic vocabulary, not as a claim about selfhood.
