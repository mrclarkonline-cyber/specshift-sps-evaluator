## What Happened

A broad outreach workflow was converted from unverified person-level emails into domain-based routing experiments.

The initial person-email layer failed badly because many contacts appeared fabricated or guessed. The strategy changed to company-level aliases and official routes.

The major operational shift was:

from: guessed individual contacts  
to: sales/contact/support aliases and official routes  
with: Wave 7 repair hold for failures

## Durable Decisions

1. No more guessed human contacts.
2. No more AI-generated executive/title/email rows as sendable contacts.
3. Use aliases and official routes only when person-level verification is absent.
4. No attachments on cold first contact.
5. One-pager only after reply or request.
6. Failed aliases move to Wave 7.
7. Wave 7 is a research queue, not deletion.
8. support@ requires a reroute-specific subject/body.
9. All senders must require exact typed confirmation.
10. All sent logs and failure logs must be preserved.
