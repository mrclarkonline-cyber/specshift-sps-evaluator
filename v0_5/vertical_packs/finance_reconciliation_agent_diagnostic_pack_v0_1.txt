Finance/Reconciliation Agent Diagnostic Pack v0.1
Internal vertical diagnostic pack draft. Not buyer-ready final copy.

Core product:
SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics

Boundary:
This vertical diagnostic pack is a pre-deployment diagnostic and architecture-stage review extension. It is not a benchmark, certification, compliance audit, runtime monitor, security tool, deployment approval, financial advice, accounting assurance, or guarantee of AI safety.

Buyer problem:
Finance and reconciliation agents can appear operationally complete while acting on stale records, unresolved ledger mismatches, partial settlement state, weak approval boundaries, or unsafe rollback assumptions.

Target workflows:
- payment reconciliation
- billing reconciliation
- ledger close support
- settlement workflow review
- invoice exception handling
- revenue operations workflow review
- ERP-to-ledger handoff review
- finance agent task-completion review

Likely failure families:
- multi-system state mismatch
- false final-state claim
- stale data acting as current
- rollback failure
- approval scope overextension
- proxy collapse

Safe use cases:
- Architecture-stage review of a finance or reconciliation agent before broader rollout.
- Pre-deployment review of stale, partial, or conflicting financial records.
- Review of handoff points between billing, payments, ERP, ledger, and human finance teams.
- Review of escalation thresholds for financial exceptions or ambiguous completion states.

Excluded uses:
- Not a financial audit.
- Not accounting assurance.
- Not compliance certification.
- Not legal, tax, or financial advice.
- Not runtime fraud detection.
- Not cybersecurity monitoring.
- Not a guarantee that workflows are safe, accurate, compliant, or deployment-ready.

Deliverables:
- Finance/reconciliation workflow diagnostic memo
- Selected synthetic scenario review summary
- State mismatch and false-completion risk map
- Evidence, provenance, and proxy-handling gap list
- Approval, handoff, escalation, and recovery-path review
- Recommended design-review next steps

Fit gate:
Green if the buyer has one concrete finance/reconciliation workflow, meaningful downside if it fails silently, bounded non-production context, and acceptance that this is diagnostic rather than audit or compliance sign-off.

Red if the buyer wants financial audit, accounting assurance, compliance certification, fraud detection, runtime monitoring, legal/tax advice, deployment approval, or guaranteed prevention of financial errors.

Failure column:
This pack stops fitting if the buyer expects SpecShift to certify financial correctness, replace audit/accounting/legal review, monitor live transactions, detect fraud in production, approve deployment, or guarantee safety, compliance, or financial accuracy.
