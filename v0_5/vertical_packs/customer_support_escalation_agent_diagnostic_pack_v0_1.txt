Customer Support Escalation Agent Diagnostic Pack v0.1
Internal vertical diagnostic pack draft. Not buyer-ready final copy.

Core product:
SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics

Boundary:
This vertical diagnostic pack is a pre-deployment diagnostic and architecture-stage review extension. It is not a benchmark, certification, compliance audit, runtime monitor, security tool, deployment approval, HR tool, clinical tool, customer-service certification, or guarantee of AI safety.

Buyer problem:
Customer support agents can appear helpful while closing cases prematurely, missing escalation triggers, treating incomplete customer context as complete, mishandling distress signals, or losing ownership across support, success, billing, technical, legal, and human escalation paths.

Target workflows:
- customer support triage
- ticket escalation routing
- complaint handling workflow
- refund or billing-dispute support workflow
- technical support handoff workflow
- retention or churn-risk escalation workflow
- customer-success escalation workflow
- support agent task-completion review

Likely failure families:
- false task-completion claim
- escalation threshold weakness
- handoff ownership gap
- distress or urgency misclassification
- proxy collapse
- scope overextension
- recovery path weakness

Safe use cases:
- Architecture-stage review of a customer support agent before broader rollout.
- Pre-deployment review of ticket triage, escalation routing, complaint handling, or support handoff workflows.
- Review of incomplete context, customer urgency, distress signals, and ambiguous ownership.
- Review of escalation thresholds for billing disputes, technical failures, high-friction complaints, or unresolved customer harm.

Excluded uses:
- Not a customer-service certification.
- Not a runtime support monitor.
- Not a replacement for human support agents, supervisors, legal teams, HR teams, clinical professionals, or trust-and-safety teams.
- Not legal advice.
- Not HR advice.
- Not clinical or mental-health support.
- Not cybersecurity monitoring.
- Not a guarantee that customer support workflows are safe, compliant, accurate, or deployment-ready.

Deliverables:
- Customer support escalation workflow diagnostic memo
- Selected synthetic scenario review summary
- Escalation threshold and false-closure risk map
- Customer context, provenance, and proxy-handling gap list
- Handoff, ownership, and recovery-path review
- Recommended design-review next steps

Fit gate:
Green if the buyer has one concrete customer support, ticket escalation, complaint, billing-dispute, or customer-success workflow, meaningful downside if it fails silently, bounded non-production context, and acceptance that this is diagnostic rather than certification or runtime monitoring.

Red if the buyer wants customer-service certification, runtime monitoring, HR tooling, legal advice, clinical support, trust-and-safety enforcement, cybersecurity assessment, compliance sign-off, deployment approval, or guaranteed prevention of customer harm.

Failure column:
This pack stops fitting if the buyer expects SpecShift to certify customer support quality, replace human support or escalation teams, provide legal/HR/clinical advice, monitor live tickets, approve deployment, or guarantee safety, compliance, retention, customer satisfaction, or support correctness.
