Healthcare Operations Agent Diagnostic Pack v0.1
Internal vertical diagnostic pack draft. Not buyer-ready final copy.

Core product:
SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics

Boundary:
This vertical diagnostic pack is a pre-deployment diagnostic and architecture-stage review extension for healthcare operations workflows. It is not a benchmark, certification, compliance audit, runtime monitor, security tool, deployment approval, clinical decision-support tool, medical device, HIPAA compliance certification, medical advice, or guarantee of AI safety.

Buyer problem:
Healthcare operations agents can appear useful while over-trusting digital records, missing physical-world constraints, losing ownership across handoffs, confusing operational support with clinical judgment, or treating incomplete scheduling, intake, capacity, or care-coordination state as complete.

Target workflows:
- patient scheduling support workflow
- referral intake routing
- bed-management or capacity coordination support
- care-coordination handoff support
- prior authorization workflow support
- patient transport or logistics coordination
- clinic operations task-routing workflow
- healthcare operations agent task-completion review

Likely failure families:
- analog-digital fracture
- professional boundary overreach
- handoff ownership gap
- stale data acting as current
- distress or urgency misclassification
- false final-state claim
- recovery path weakness

Safe use cases:
- Architecture-stage review of a healthcare operations agent before broader rollout.
- Pre-deployment review of scheduling, referral intake, patient access, care-coordination, logistics, or administrative routing workflows.
- Review of stale operational records, digital-physical mismatch, ambiguous handoffs, and escalation thresholds.
- Review of whether a workflow clearly separates administrative support from clinical or professional judgment.

Excluded uses:
- Not clinical decision support.
- Not medical advice.
- Not a medical device.
- Not diagnosis, triage, treatment, or patient-specific clinical judgment.
- Not HIPAA compliance certification.
- Not legal, billing, coding, or reimbursement advice.
- Not runtime patient-safety monitoring.
- Not cybersecurity monitoring.
- Not a guarantee that healthcare operations workflows are safe, compliant, accurate, or deployment-ready.
- Not a replacement for clinical, legal, compliance, privacy, security, or healthcare operations teams.

Deliverables:
- Healthcare operations workflow diagnostic memo
- Selected synthetic scenario review summary
- Analog-digital fracture and false-completion risk map
- Operational evidence, provenance, and stale-state gap list
- Handoff, escalation, professional-boundary, and recovery-path review
- Recommended design-review next steps

Fit gate:
Green if the buyer has one concrete healthcare operations workflow, the workflow is administrative or operational rather than clinical decision-making, silent failure has meaningful operational consequence, bounded non-production context is available, and the buyer understands this is diagnostic rather than clinical, medical, compliance, or deployment approval.

Red if the buyer wants clinical decision support, medical advice, diagnosis, treatment triage, HIPAA certification, legal advice, billing/coding advice, runtime patient-safety monitoring, cybersecurity assessment, compliance sign-off, deployment approval, or guaranteed prevention of patient harm.

Failure column:
This pack stops fitting if the buyer expects SpecShift to make or validate clinical decisions, provide medical advice, certify HIPAA or regulatory compliance, monitor live patient workflows, replace clinical/privacy/legal/security review, approve deployment, or guarantee safety, compliance, access quality, medical correctness, or operational correctness.
