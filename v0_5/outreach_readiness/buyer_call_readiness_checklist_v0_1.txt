Buyer Call Readiness Checklist v0.1
Internal operating checklist. Not buyer-facing copy.

Before call:
- Open the website and confirm the focused diagnostic pilot language is visible.
- Have the focused diagnostic pilot one-pager ready if asked.
- Keep the standing boundary line visible.
- Do not discuss upper-tier pricing, strategic license, certification, benchmark, runtime monitoring, compliance, security, or safety guarantees.
- Aim to identify one concrete workflow, not sell the whole stack.

During call:
1. What AI-agent workflow are they considering, prototyping, or already testing?
2. What systems or data sources does the workflow touch?
3. What inputs are treated as trusted?
4. What actions can the agent recommend, initiate, or complete?
5. Where are human approvals, handoffs, or review points?
6. What happens when data is stale, incomplete, conflicting, or low-fidelity?
7. How is task completion verified?
8. What escalation, rollback, or recovery path exists?

Fit gate:
Green:
One concrete workflow, meaningful downside if silent failure occurs, bounded context available, buyer understands diagnostic-only scope.

Yellow:
Workflow exists but context, owner, or boundary is unclear.

Red:
Buyer wants certification, compliance sign-off, runtime monitoring, security testing, deployment approval, legal/clinical/HR decision-making, free broad consulting, or guaranteed safety.

After email is collected:
Run:

cd ~/specshift_terminal_intelligence && python3 v0_5/call_companion/specshift_call_companion.py

Choose mode 2 if call notes are being captured live.
Choose mode 3 if only a follow-up email draft is needed.

Default attachment:
Focused diagnostic pilot one-pager PDF.

Failure column:
This checklist fails if the call drifts into broad free consulting, unsupported pricing, certification, compliance approval, runtime monitoring, security tooling, legal/clinical/HR advice, deployment approval, or guaranteed safety.
