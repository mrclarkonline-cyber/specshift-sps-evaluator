SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics
Internal Sales Narrative Draft v0.1
Not a benchmark. Not certification. Not a compliance product. Not a runtime control system.

Positioning:
Pre-Deployment Architectural Logic Stress-Testing and Constraint Mapping Framework for Agentic Workflows

One-sentence description:
SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics helps teams stress-test proposed AI agent workflows before deployment by mapping where automated reasoning, evidence handling, scope boundaries, and escalation logic may break under synthetic edge-case review.

Short pitch:
AI agents can sound confident while acting on weak evidence, stale records, unclear authority, or incomplete handoffs. SpecShift provides a pre-deployment diagnostic review that surfaces those failure patterns before broader rollout. The output is not a certification or runtime control system; it is a structured internal map of where the workflow appears stable under synthetic stress and where design guardrails may be needed.

Buyer problem:
- AI agents may treat forwarded documents, screenshots, summaries, or generated history as decision-grade evidence.
- Agent workflows may exceed intended scope when approval boundaries are vague.
- Multi-system workflows may act on stale records, mismatched states, or unverified completion claims.
- Teams may lack clear escalation thresholds for when an AI workflow should pause, route, or require human review.

Core deliverables:
- Adversarial Stress-Test Execution Report: Findings from simulated edge-case and failure-mode scenarios.
- Limitation & Boundary Map: Structured inventory of where the AI workflow's reasoning may degrade under synthetic stress conditions.
- Agentic Scope & Logic Alignment Review: Architecture-stage review of scope boundaries, data freshness assumptions, system-record consistency, and handoff logic.
- Escalation & Human-in-the-Loop Routing Matrix: Proposed review thresholds and escalation paths for cases where the workflow should pause, route, or require human review.

Pilot framing:
A bounded paid diagnostic engagement focused on one high-value AI agent workflow. SpecShift reviews the workflow against an internal adversarial stress-test suite, maps operational boundaries, identifies evidence-handling and state-alignment risks, and delivers architecture-stage review outputs for engineering, product, and risk teams.

Pricing posture:
Priced as a professional diagnostic engagement rather than a SaaS subscription. The pilot fee covers structured stress-test execution, limitation mapping, and delivery of architecture-stage review outputs. This supports premium pilot pricing as a risk-reduction and design-review engagement without implying a software guarantee, compliance sign-off, or runtime control layer.

Claim boundary:
This narrative stops fitting if it is used to imply certification, compliance sign-off, runtime monitoring, deployment readiness, or guaranteed AI safety. It should be used only to describe a pre-deployment diagnostic and architecture-stage review engagement.
