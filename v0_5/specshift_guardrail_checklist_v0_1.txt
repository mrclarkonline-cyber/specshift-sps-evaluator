SpecShift Guardrail Checklist v0.1
Internal review checklist. Not a validated scoring instrument.

A practical pre-deployment and architecture-stage review aid to help teams identify where reliability scaffolds may be weak in agentic workflows and high-uncertainty tasks. It supports clearer provenance, better boundary setting, and more responsible human-AI collaboration.

1. Input Provenance & Evidence Handling
- Is every major input clearly labeled by source type, such as user-provided, system-generated, external document, or proxy?
- Are assumptions about data freshness, completeness, and authority stated explicitly?
- Is there a clear separation between raw input and model inference?
- Are low-fidelity proxies such as screenshots, summaries, or forwarded emails flagged with appropriate caveats?
Failure column: If provenance is not labeled or proxies are treated as high-fidelity evidence, the review should flag elevated risk of misplaced confidence.

2. Claim Boundary & Limitation Mapping
- Does the output clearly state what is known, what is inferred, and what remains unknown?
- Are scope limitations and boundary conditions explicitly listed?
- Is there a visible failure column: specific conditions where the recommendation or framework would break or become misleading?
- Are overclaims such as universality, guarantees, or completeness avoided?
Failure column: If the output presents strong claims without visible boundaries or failure conditions, the scaffold is insufficient for high-stakes use.

3. Agentic Workflow Logic Alignment
- Are permission levels, data freshness, and policy constraints explicitly checked before action?
- Is there clear handling for stale data, multi-system mismatches, and state drift?
- Are handoffs between agents or to humans well-defined with ownership transfer?
- Is there protection against silent policy violations or approval scope overextension?
Failure column: If agentic actions can bypass defined controls or create ambiguous ownership, workflow integrity risk is high.

4. Human Review Triggers & Escalation
- Are clear triggers defined for when human review or approval is required?
- Is the escalation path explicit for high-value, high-risk, or ambiguous cases?
- Does the system avoid over-delegation in areas with meaningful consequences?
- Is there a defined recovery or rollback path when things go wrong?
Failure column: If human review triggers are vague or missing for material actions, the design carries elevated operational risk.

5. Distress, Safety & Moral Disagreement Handling
- If user distress or emotional impact is evident, is stabilization prioritized before analysis?
- Are moral or values-based disagreements diagnosed clearly rather than reduced to technical framing?
- Is there a responsible hand-off to human professionals when distress, safety, legal, or ethical stakes rise?
- Does the response avoid performing roles outside its proper scope, such as HR, clinical, or legal functions?
Failure column: If distress is present but analysis is prioritized, or if the system performs professional roles it should not, the response pattern falls outside appropriate review boundaries.

Use cases:
- Pre-deployment architecture review of agentic systems
- Internal evaluation of workflow reliability scaffolds
- Structured critique of proposed AI-assisted decision processes
- Team alignment on boundary-setting practices

Not for:
- This is not a certification, compliance audit, security assessment, or deployment approval tool.
- It is not intended to replace professional legal, technical, or risk assessment processes.
- It should not be used as a public benchmark or vendor comparison instrument.

Checklist failure column: This checklist itself stops fitting if it is treated as a complete or authoritative evaluation method rather than a lightweight internal thinking aid. It is most useful when applied as a conversation starter and gap identifier, not as a scoring system.
