Strategic Evaluation Claim-Boundary Review v0.1
Internal claim-boundary review. Not buyer-ready final copy.

Overall verdict:
The Strategic Evaluation Package is commercially strong and appropriately premium, but it must keep visible boundaries around diagnostic use, synthetic stress review, non-certification, and non-runtime status before any outreach or executive PDF is created.

Allowed claims:
- SpecShift provides a pre-deployment diagnostic review for high-consequence AI-agent workflows.
- SpecShift uses synthetic stress scenarios to identify candidate failure modes in evidence handling, scope boundaries, handoffs, escalation logic, and recovery paths.
- SpecShift outputs structured internal maps and review materials that can support architecture-stage decision-making.
- SpecShift can help teams identify where a workflow may need additional design guardrails before scaling.

Claims to avoid:
- Do not claim certification, compliance approval, safety guarantee, deployment readiness, or benchmark status.
- Do not imply runtime monitoring, runtime protection, access-control enforcement, HR decision tooling, legal advice, or cybersecurity scanning.
- Do not claim validated performance, incident reduction, or production reliability without evidence.
- Do not imply that synthetic stress review proves real-world behavior.

Phrases to keep:
- pre-deployment diagnostic
- architecture-stage review
- synthetic stress review
- candidate failure modes
- structured internal map
- review thresholds
- recovery paths
- bounded paid diagnostic engagement

Safe standing boundary line:
SpecShift Architectural Logic Diagnostics is a pre-deployment diagnostic and architecture-stage review process. It is not a benchmark, certification, compliance audit, runtime monitor, security tool, deployment approval, or guarantee of AI safety.
